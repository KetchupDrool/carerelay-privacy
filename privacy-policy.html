Alvin
Handoff notes for dementia caregivers.

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: July 10, 2026  ·  Version 1.0

The short version: Alvin stores everything on your device. We collect no personal data, and there are no accounts, no cloud sync, no ads, and no trackers. The only outside connection is an anonymous, one-time lookup to a public government drug database when you scan a medication — no personal information is ever included in that request.

1. Who We Are

Alvin is an independent iOS app that helps family caregivers hand off care notes, medication schedules, behavior logs, and appointment details for a loved one living with dementia.

Questions about this policy: mtcombatmedic@gmail.com

2. Data We Collect

We collect no personal data.

Alvin does not require an account. We do not ask for your name, email address, phone number, or location.

Everything you enter — caregiver profiles, medication lists, behavior logs, appointment notes, and care mode settings — is stored exclusively on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework, with Apple's complete file protection encryption applied. It never leaves your device unless you explicitly export or share it.

3. Data We Do Not Collect

We do not collect, store, or transmit any of the following:

  • Names, email addresses, or contact information
  • Location data or GPS coordinates
  • Health or medical records
  • Device identifiers or advertising IDs
  • Usage analytics or crash reports
  • Behavioral tracking or session data
  • Financial information

4. On-Device Storage Only

Every piece of information you log about the person in your care lives entirely on your iPhone or iPad. We have no servers that receive your data. There is no cloud sync and no backend database.

When you delete the app, all of your data is deleted with it. We strongly recommend keeping your device backed up (e.g., via encrypted iCloud or iTunes/Finder backup) since we have no copy to restore.

5. Camera Use (Medication Scanning)

Alvin can use your camera to scan a medication label or barcode so you can add medication details faster. The camera is used only in the moment of scanning — no photo or video is saved, stored, or transmitted. You review and confirm all scanned details before anything is saved to your device.

6. Medication Lookup (openFDA)

When you scan a medication's National Drug Code (NDC), Alvin sends only that code to the U.S. FDA's public openFDA API (api.fda.gov) to retrieve the medication's name and details. No personal information, caregiver names, or care recipient data is included in this request — only the drug code itself. This lookup is governed by the FDA's own terms as a public government service.

7. PDF Export and Sharing

Alvin lets you generate PDF reports — such as appointment prep sheets and care mode summaries — to share with a doctor, family member, or another caregiver. These reports are generated entirely on your device and are never sent to any server. You control where they go: you initiate the share, and iOS handles delivery via Messages, Mail, AirDrop, Files, or any app you choose. We never see the contents of anything you export or share.

8. In-App Purchases

Alvin may offer optional premium features or subscriptions through Apple's App Store. All purchases are processed entirely by Apple. We do not collect or store your payment information — we receive only a confirmation from Apple that a purchase was completed, no financial details.

9. A Note on Sensitive Information

We know the information you enter in Alvin — about a family member's health, behavior, and care needs — is deeply personal. That's exactly why Alvin is built to keep it on your device and out of our hands entirely. We have no ability to view, access, or recover any data you enter, because we never receive it.

Because all data stays on your device and is never transmitted to us, Alvin is not a HIPAA-covered entity or business associate, and HIPAA's data-sharing obligations do not apply to how we handle information in the app. You remain responsible for how you store, back up, and share exported reports.

10. Third-Party Services

Alvin does not integrate with any third-party analytics, advertising, or data-sharing services. There are no SDKs from Facebook, Google, Firebase, Mixpanel, or similar platforms in the app.

The only third-party services involved are Apple (App Store, StoreKit, on-device Speech framework) and the FDA's public openFDA API described in Section 7, each governed by its own privacy policy.

11. Security

Because your data never leaves your device, security is tied to your device's own security. We recommend using a passcode, Face ID, or Touch ID. If your device is lost or stolen, your data is protected by iOS device encryption.

12. Children's Privacy

Alvin is intended for adult caregivers and is not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect information from anyone under 13.

13. Your Rights

Because we collect no personal data, there is nothing for us to access, correct, or delete on our end. All of your data is on your device, under your control. You can delete it at any time by deleting entries in the app or deleting the app itself.

14. Changes to This Policy

If we update this policy, we will post the new version here and update the effective date. If the changes are material, we will notify you within the app. Continued use of Alvin after a policy change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
